Our Legacy

A Brotherhood of the Hunt

Nestled deep within the ancient oak and pine of the Ashfield Valley, Huntsman's Hollow has stood as a sanctuary for the discerning hunter since 1947. Founded by twelve men who believed that true sport demands not just skill, but reverence — for land, for quarry, and for tradition — our club remains a place apart from the modern world.

Our grounds span over 4,200 acres of managed wilderness, including three private lakes, eleven miles of river frontage, and some of the most productive upland bird habitat in the region. Whether pursuing whitetail, wild turkey, or the elusive ruffed grouse, our members find no finer ground.

October 2024 · Grounds Report
Whitetail Population Reaches Record High After Three-Year Conservation Effort
Following a rigorous three-year habitat management programme overseen by Head Warden Dale Pruitt, camera trap surveys now confirm over 340 individual whitetail deer on our managed grounds — the highest population recorded in the club's history. The new food plot rotations along the eastern ridgeline have proven particularly effective, with velvet bucks consistently photographed in numbers not seen since the 1980s.
